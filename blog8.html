Tigers: The Largest Cats in the World

1. What is special about white tigers?

White tiger facts show us how these beautiful tigers have more unique characteristics when compared to other tigers than just the color of their fur. They are of course a majestic white shade with black stripes but they also have a distinct pink nose and blue eyes. They are often much larger than other tigers, both at birth and as they mature. A white tiger is usually considered to be an adult tiger between two and three years of age.

2. Quick Facts about White Tiger!

1. Scientific Name: Panthera tigris.

2. Kingdom: Animalia.

3. Family: Felidae.

4. Habitat: Dense jungle and swamp.

5. Diet: Carnivore.

6. Weight: 309 lbs to 660 lbs.

7. Top Speed: 60 miles per hour.

8. Life Span: 10-20 years.

9. Average Litter Size: 3 cubs.

10. Endangered: Yes.

3. Where was the first white tiger found and when?

The first white tiger spotted in India was in 1951 In 1951, Maharaja Martand Singh spotted a white tiger cub in Rewa, a village in Madhya Pradesh for the first time.
The first white tiger was spotted in Madhya Pradesh's Vindhya region, in which the Satna district falls, in 1915. The rare breed of the big cat, which was caught for the first time, died in 1920. In 1951, a white tiger cub, named Mohan, was captured.
There are only around 200 white tigers left in the world, according to the Indian Tiger Welfare Society.

5. What is the rarest tiger?!

Sumatran tigers are the rarest and smallest subspecies of tiger in the world and are currently classed as critically endangered.

5. What are 3 interesting facts about tigers?!

Tigers are the largest cat species in the world and the third-largest carnivore on land--only polar and brown bears are larger. An adult Amur or Siberian tiger (the largest subspecies) can weigh up to 660 pounds. The Sumatran tiger is the smallest, with males only weighing up to 310 pounds.
